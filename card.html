Kangaroo

Kangaroo gives birth to highly immature young that nurse and develop in a pouch

  • Class: Mammalia
  • Geographical location: Australia
  • Life span: 12 to 18 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Grasslands

Kangaroos stand on large rear legs, using their long tail for added support. They have small front legs, with handlike paws that lack an opposable thumb. Hopping is their most unusual characteristic. As marsupials, kangaroos nurse their young (called joeys) in a pouch.
